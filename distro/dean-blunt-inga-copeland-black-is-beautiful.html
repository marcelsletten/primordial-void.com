Dean Blunt and Inga Copeland’s hazy 2012 classic Black Is Beautiful is available on CD in the U.S once again, and these are some of the very last copies you’ll find anywhere. It just might be the duo’s most fully realized statement in their brief discography, and a true testament to the ultra-lo-fi, dub- and synthpop-inspired Hype Williams sound before the elusive pair eventually split. We have only 12 copies left in stock—this is guaranteed to sell out instantly.

From the original press release:

“After three albums for as many labels, Dean Blunt and Inga Copeland, also of the group Hype Williams, release their debut Hyperdub LP, Black Is Beautiful. Amid rumours circulating, and confirmed by their "advisor" Denna Frances, the album was recorded during a tremulous period for the two : Inga Copelands Arsenal girls tryouts, Dean's 'Waschbrgate' trial (he was implicated in being behind a one night robbing spree across 16 taxidermists based in London and the South East, allegedly only for racoons) and his subsequent conversion to Nation of Islam upon being acquitted. Black Is Beautiful is laced with their signature hazy glow and is full of crunched eurovision lullabies, kinetic drum machine jams, unsettling funk, smeared walls of synth, moments of unadulterated opiated bliss, mogadon rap, infinite coughs and insights into the odd sexual behaviour of crackers.”
